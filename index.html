Biriyani

Soak 300g basmati rice in warm water, then wash in cold until the water runs clear.Heat 25g butter in a saucepan and cook 1 finely sliced large onion with 1 bay leaf, 3 cardamom pods and 1 small cinnamon stick for 10 mins.Sprinkle in 1 tsp turmeric, then add 4 chicken breasts, cut into large chunks, and 4 tbsp curry paste. Cook until aromatic.Stir the rice into the pan with 85g raisins, then pour over 850ml chicken stock.Place a tight-fitting lid on the pan and bring to a hard boil, then lower the heat t a minimum and cook the rice for another 5 mins.Turn off the heat and leave for 10 mins. Stir well, mixing through 15g chopped coriander. To serve, scatter over the leaves of the remaining 15g coriander and 2 tbsp toasted almonds.

Hilsha Fry

To make this delicious curry, wash the fish and slice it into pieces.Once done, marinate the pieces using salt and turmeric. Keep it aside for some time.Then, heat some oil in the pan and shallow fry the marinated fish pieces until they turn golden.In another pan, heat the remaining oil, and add the cumin seeds. Silt the green chillies and add to the oil. Keep stirring. Add the fried fish pieces to the oil, then pour in the boiled water. Now, add the salt and turmeric powder as per your taste. Bring it to a boil and let it cook for a few minutes. Serve your Bengali fish curry hot with steamed rice.

Borhani

Chop the mint leaves along with the stems and then make a paste. Mix the mustard powder with water. Add it to the yogurt. Beat the mixture well in a blender. If it is too thick add water accordingly.Add all the other ingredients to the blender and blend well. When blended well, pour it in a jar and refrigerate.Take the drink out of the refrigerator right before serving. Serve cool. You can serve each glass with a sprinkle of coriander leaves for decoration and cumin powder for flavor.One should not store Borhani more than three days
